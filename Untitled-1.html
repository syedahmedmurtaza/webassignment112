Wearing a facemask is an effective way to reduce the transmission of airborne diseases, including COVID-19. Ensure the mask covers both your nose and mouth fully and fits snugly against the sides of your face without gaps. Wash your hands before putting on the mask, after removing it, and avoid touching it while wearing it. Use masks appropriate for the situation, such as cloth masks for general use and N95 respirators for high-risk settings. Wash reusable masks after each use and dispose of single-use masks properly. For those with respiratory conditions, consult a healthcare provider about the best type of mask. Remember, masks are most effective when combined with other preventive measures like social distancing and vaccination.
